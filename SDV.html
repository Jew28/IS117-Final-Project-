Stardew Valley

Developer: ConcernedApe (Eric Barone)

Release Date: February 26th 2016

Steam Deck Rating: Verified (one of the top played games on Steam Deck)

Stardew Valley’s gameplay is deceptively simple at first. You start with a small farm, a few tools, and 1500 gold. Farming is what you start with first, with the game beginning at the start of Spring. Spring is all about planting and getting started. Summer brings in high‑value crops like blueberries and Melons, with some players making moves to explore the cave near the mountains, fall is cozy with pumpkins and cranberries, and winter forces you to focus on mining, fishing, or animal care since crops won’t grow. (unless you manage to find Powdermelon seeds) Each season feels different, and planning ahead becomes part of the fun. The caves add a whole other layer. The mines start off manageable, but Skull Cavern is...difficult. You could drop multiple levels at once, fighting monsters, and scraping by with 1 HP while hoping you don’t lose all your loot (You probably will, if it's a bad luck day) It’s risky, but the rewards are always worth it. After all, its go big or go home. Especially when money is involved. And thats not even all you can do...you can build relationships, build up the town, even unlock a beach resort! The world (or town) is your oyster, and you'll more than enjoy it
