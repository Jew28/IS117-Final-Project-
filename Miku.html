Hatsune Miku: Project Diva Mega Mix +

Developer: SEGA / Crypton Future Media

Release Date: May 15 2020

Steam Deck Rating: Playable (due to text being small, the game runs completely fine)

Hatsune Miku: Project Diva MegaMix+ is pure rhythm‑game energy. An expanded PC port of the original Nintendo Switch port, that in my opinion, is the more definitive addition of the game The core gameplay is all about hitting notes in time with Vocaloid tracks, and it’s way more addictive than I expected. Each song has its own chart, and difficulty ramps up fast (Normal is Hard, Hard is Extra Hard, and Extra Hard is impossible) The game lets you choose between the Megamix style and the Future Tone styling for the models. Personally, I stick with the Future Tone Models The DLC packs enhance the base game by adding tons of classic songs from older Project Diva titles. Beyond just hitting notes, there’s also a layer of customisation — unlocking cosmetics, outfits, and accessories for Miku and the other characters. It’s not necessary to buy DLC for cosmetics since you can earn currency in‑game, but it’s nice to have everything unlocked if you want to experiment. On Steam Deck, the game is marked “Playable” instead of “Verified,” mostly because of small text and occasional keyboard input prompts. But once you tweak accessibility settings (like zooming in on button labels), the rhythm gameplay itself runs smoothly. I personally like playing the game with an Xbox controller, mainly to allow for longer play sessions At its core, Mega Mix + is the culmination of the Project Diva series, showcasing its history and the reasons why the franchise has defined rhythm games for years
